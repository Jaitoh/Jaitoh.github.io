[Reading] "Reading 200330 Choosing the greater of two goods Neural currencies for valuation and decision making"

Posted on 2020-03-30 Edited on 2021-07-23 In Reading Views:

value: intuition -> quantitative measurement and prediction
LIP guide behavior responses
DLPFC computes values regarding reward

Abstract$^2$:

value out of the realm of intuition and into the realm of quantitative measurement and prediction
LIP guide behavior responses / DLPFC computes values regarding reward (strong connections in between)
choice behavior should contain two different NS fields: perception and congnition / reward and motivation

To make adaptive decisions, animals must evaluate the costs and benefits of available options.

The nascent field of neuroeconomics has set itself the ambitious goal of understanding the brain mechanisms that are responsible for these evaluative processes.

A series of recent neurophysiological studies in monkeys has begun to address this challenge using novel methods to manipulate and measure an animal’s internal valuation of competing alternatives.

By emphasizing the behavioural mechanisms and neural signals that mediate decision making under conditions of uncertainty, these studies might lay the foundation for an emerging neurobiology of choice behaviour.

The form of our review recapitulates (概括) the development of this topic.

We begin by summarizing the progress that has been made during the past decade in uncovering the neural correlates of simple perceptual decisions.

We then introduce our primary focus — the interaction between decision making and reward — by exploring some key concepts that relate to the neurobiology of reward and motivation.

Finally, we turn to recent attempts that have been made to expand the neurobiology of decision making to account for the powerful influences of reward history and expectation on choice behaviour.

Question:

As known, DLPFC is more related to the region of cognition and LIP is more close to the motor area in the brain.

It is found that both would compute the value which influences the final decision and movement and there is a strong connection between DLPFC and LIP.

Is there any experiment or research that investigates the firing sequence of these two areas and the difference between these two values to the final decision (movement)?

From my intuition:

  • cognitive signals should fire before the motor signals which means DLPFC fires in advance to LIP.
  • for decision or movement making, the cognition/reward region (DLPFC) should have a higher weighting compared with the motor-decision region (LIP). A higher weighting is supposed to have the biggest influence on the final decision/movement.
